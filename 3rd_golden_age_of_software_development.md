# 3rd Golden age of software engineering

> The entire history of software engineering is one of rising levels of abstraction

### Static and dynamic forces that weigh upon software engineering

As with other engineerings, which balance external and internal forces to build something, so does software

- You can't pass information faster than the speed of light (physical limitation)
- We can't build anything software solution larger than what the hardware handles (hardware limitation)
- Can I get enough people to do what I need to do? (human limitation)

## The 1st golden age of software engineering: Software being decoupled from hardware

> 1st golden age: Software becoming an industry into itself

late 40's to late 70's

- Software as an entity onto itself has its term coined 70/80 years ago
  - 1st generation: focused largely on:
    - mathematical needs
    - automation of existing business processes
- Initially, the plugs being connected to ENIAC, for instance, were programmable, but weren't software
  - The program was deeply attached to the machine
- Assembly language was the first software
- Engineering, business, and economic decision: detach software from hardware
- 1st abstraction: algorithmic abstraction
- We started seeing the division of labor on the field. We had people who would `_____`
  - analyze the system
  - program the system
  - key punch the solutions
- cost of machine > human cost

- Even though the center of this movement was in banks, IBM, and things related to business, at the fringe, there were other things happening
  - medical systems
  - weather forecasting
  - defense systems
    - there was the need to create distributed systems of real-time nature
